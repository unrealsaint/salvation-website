# Delusion Chamber (Chamber of Delusion) — Player Guide

How to enter the **Delusion Chamber** / **Chamber of Delusion** seals on Oasis Salvation, how **rooms** and **Aenkinel** work, **soul crystal** rules, and what **loot** to expect. Server-wide drop bonuses may still apply on top of what is listed here.

---

## Overview

The Delusion Chamber is a **private dungeon** made of several **rooms** you rotate through, with a chance to land in a **boss room** where **Aenkinel** (Invader of Dream) waits. There are **four smaller seals** and **two larger** ones.

| Category | Which seals | Time inside | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| **Smaller seals** | **Eastern**, **Western**, **Southern**, **Northern** | **40 minutes** | Same general idea and similar rewards on each. |
| **Larger seals** | **Great Seal** (sometimes called the square seal) and **Tower** | **60 minutes** | More time, heavier rewards, extra rules (see re-entry below). |

**Pets and summons cannot be used** inside any of these dungeons.

---

## Who can enter

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Level** | **80–85** |
| **Party** | **2–9** players |
| **Summons** | **Not allowed** |
| **Starting the run** | Talk to the **guardian** at the seal you want; the **party leader** is the one who actually enters the instance for the group. |

### Smaller seals (four directions)

- **40-minute** limit inside the dungeon.

### Great Seal and Tower

- **60-minute** limit.
- **Re-entry:** on this server the larger seals are tied to a **daily reset** (typically early morning server time, often around **6:30 AM**). After you finish a run, you may need to wait until the next reset before that character can enter again—watch in-game messages or your instance reuse timer.

---

## Delusion Chamber Invitation (moving rooms)

- You need a **Delusion Chamber Invitation** to request a **manual** room change from a **Delusion Manager** NPC inside the dungeon.
- When the party enters, old invitations are cleaned up and **one** invitation is given to the **party leader** so they can pay for moves.
- Each manual move **uses one** invitation and works like another **room roll** (same as the automatic rotation).
- Normal play expects a **full party** (2–9); special solo entry may exist depending on server settings.

---

## Rooms and boss room

### Automatic moves

Every **several minutes** (roughly **8–10** real-time minutes between cycles) the floor **shakes**, then the party is sent to **another room** at random among the normal rooms.

### Manual moves

The **party leader** talks to a **Delusion Manager** and spends **one invitation** to trigger a room change **immediately**.

### Chance to enter the boss room

Each time a room change happens (automatic or manual), there is a **15%** chance the party is sent to the **boss room** (Aenkinel) instead of a normal room—**same rate on all six seals**, small and large.

If the roll fails, you go to a **random normal** room (not the same one you just left).

On the **larger** seals, once you are **already** in the boss room, further automatic shuffling stops until you deal with the boss or leave; **killing Aenkinel** finishes that large run.

## Boss: Aenkinel

| Where you entered | Boss level (approx.) | Soul crystal note |
|-------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Eastern** seal | 81 | See soul crystal section: **cannot** award the **highest** stage step available on the big seals. |
| **Western** seal | 81 | Same as Eastern. |
| **Southern** seal | 81 | Same as Eastern. |
| **Northern** seal | 81 | Same as Eastern. |
| **Great Seal** | 82 | Allows the **extra** high-tier soul crystal step that the four small seals do **not**. |
| **Tower** seal | 84 | Same soul crystal rules as Great Seal; loot differs (see drops). |

On the **four small seals**, after the boss dies the instance continues with the usual **chest / box** style reward moment you see in those seals. On **Great Seal** and **Tower**, defeating Aenkinel **ends** that dungeon run.

## Soul crystal leveling (Aenkinel only)

Soul crystals only interact with these bosses if you are on the classic **Enchant Your Weapon** quest line (the one that lets weapons absorb from bosses). If you never started it—or the game no longer considers it active—**no absorb** will happen for you.

### Rules

1. **Quest:** keep **Enchant Your Weapon** active and valid for your character if you want a chance to level a crystal here.
2. **One crystal:** carry **exactly one** absorbable soul crystal in your inventory when the boss dies. If you hold **two or more**, the game treats it as **interference** and **nobody in that situation gets** a clean absorb from that kill (you may see a “resonation” style failure message).
3. **Which seals for which stage:**  
   - On the **four small seals**, the boss can only push your crystal through the **middle of the high tiers** (roughly **stages 11 through 14** in the usual red/green/blue progression—match this to the **stage written on your crystal**). The **last** of those middle steps (**stage 15** in the same numbering) **does not** happen here.  
   - On **Great Seal** and **Tower**, the same lower range applies **plus** that **final** high-tier step (**stage 15**).

### Odds and who is picked

- **Success chance:** **20%** per eligible attempt that the crystal **levels up** to the next item (normal progression).
- **Who rolls:** the server picks **one random party member** who qualifies (quest OK, single crystal, correct stage for that seal). It is **not** guaranteed to be the leader—coordinate who holds the crystal.

If the roll fails, you **keep** your current crystal. These Aenkinel entries do **not** use the separate **cursed** crystal branch.

### Tips

- Agree **before the pull** who carries the crystal, and make sure **only** that person has one—or accept that **random** eligible member might get the roll.
- For the **last** high-tier stage (**15**), farm **Great Seal** or **Tower**, not the four small seals.

## Drops — what to expect

Drop tables use **several loot buckets**. First the game decides **whether** a bucket fires (each bucket has its own chance). **Inside** a bucket that fired, items compete by **weight**—higher weight means **more common relative to other items in that same bucket**, not a straight “percent on the boss.”

Server **raid drop** multipliers can still change how often you see loot in practice.

### Four small seals (Eastern / Western / Southern / Northern)

**Jewelry bucket** (fires fairly often)

- **Common within bucket:** Dynasty ring / earring / necklace **gemstones** in small stacks.  
- **Much rarer:** sealed Dynasty **ring**, **earring**, or **necklace** themselves.

**Weapons bucket** (fires a bit less often than jewelry)

- **Common within bucket:** **weapon pieces** for Dynasty sword / blade / phantom.  
- **Very rare:** full **Dynasty Sword**, **Dynasty Blade**, or **Dynasty Phantom**.

**Scrolls bucket** (less often than the two above)

- Mostly **S-grade armor enchant scrolls**, sometimes **S-grade weapon enchant scrolls**, rarely **blessed weapon S enchants**.

No **Guiding Tea Leaves** bundle on these four bosses in the current list.

---

### Great Seal (larger dungeon)

Same **idea** as the small seals but **larger stacks** of gemstones and pieces, and **extra** buckets:

- **Jewelry bucket** — same items as small seals, **bigger** minimum and maximum counts on gemstones.  
- **Weapons bucket** — Dynasty weapons and pieces again, **larger** piece stacks.  
- **Rare weapon bucket** — very small chance for **Astaroth** (dual swords).  
- **Scrolls bucket** — same scroll types; armor scrolls can drop in **slightly higher count** than on the small seals.  
- **Always** — a separate roll gives **Guiding Tea Leaves** (a few per kill).

---

### Tower seal (larger dungeon)

- **Jewelry bucket** — same pattern as **Great Seal** (heavy gemstones, sealed jewelry rarer).  
- **Weapons bucket** — **Icarus** line (**Sawsword**, **Spirit**, **Heavy Arms**) and their **pieces**, with chunky stack sizes on the pieces. **No** Astaroth bucket here.  
- **Scrolls bucket** — same scroll mix style as Great Seal (weapon / armor / blessed weapon; armor can be **multiple**).  
- **Always** — **Guiding Tea Leaves**, usually **one more** on average than Great Seal.

---

## Quick checklist

1. Party **2–9**, levels **80–85**, **no summons**.  
2. Leader talks to the correct **seal guardian** to start.  
3. Leader keeps **invitations** for manual room moves; every move rolls **15%** boss room.  
4. Soul crystals: **Enchant Your Weapon** active, **one** crystal on the person you want rolled, **stage 15** only on **Great** or **Tower**.  
5. Expect **bigger** Dynasty rolls, **tea leaves**, and either **Astaroth** (Great) or **Icarus** weapons (Tower) on the large seals.
